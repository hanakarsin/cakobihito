Obadiah Mailafia's Confession About Boko Haram Sponsors

A former presidential candidate, Obadiah Mailafia was confirmed dead on Sunday morning.

Before his demise, Mailafia who was also a former Deputy Governor of the Central Bank of Nigeria, made some heart blowing confessions about the existence of dreaded Boko Haram terrorist groups in the country.

Upon hearing of Mailafia’s death a few moments ago, Naija News is challenged to recall one of the late economists’ comments due to its relation to the regretful security situations in the country right now.

Mailafia had said in August last year that a sitting governor in the northern region of Nigeria is the commander of Boko Haram.

The comment then, stirred up a barrage of reactions and the statesman was at some point invited for questions.

Mailafiya who was reacting to the insecurity issues affecting South Kaduna stated boldly during an interview on Radio station, Nigerian Info that the information which he hold on to was revealed by the former high commanders of the group who have repented.

Unrepentant with his stand even after interrogations, Mailafia maintained that Boko Haram and the bandits are the same things.

“They have a sophisticated network. During the lockdown, their planes were moving up and down as if there was no lockdown,” Mailafia noted.

When asked if the government was unwilling or unable to protect the people of Kaduna, Mailafiya said; “In fact, it is not only unable, but it is also unwilling and we have good reason to feel that they are part and parcel of the killers let me make some revelations to you.

“How will a government just say it is revenge killings and so on, then you leave it there because it is revenge killings and so on. The body language of this administration, the body language of the state government shows clearly that they have a hand in the killings, no doubt about it.

“Let me make some revelations because some of us also have our intelligence networks, have met with some of the bandits, we have met with some of their high commanders, one or two who have repented, they have sat down with us, not once, not twice. They told us one of the Northern governors is the Commander of Boko Haram in Nigeria”.

Mailafia died today at the National Hospital in Abuja.

Whether his claims are true or are just mere speculations, the security situations in the country are far from good.
