Education system in Kenya

The new education system in Kenya replaces the old education system. The old education system provides eight years of primary education, four years of secondary education and four years of university. The new education system provides two years of pre-primary school, six years of lower and upper primary school, three years of junior secondary school, three years of senior secondary school and three years of higher education.

The level of new education system in Kenya is briefly explained below:

  • Pre Primary years of education: Pre primary education level includes two years- pre primary 1 and pre primary 2. The children come under 4 and 5 years are joining pre primary school.
  • Lower primary education: To complete lower education level it takes 3 years. The age criteria come between 6 and 8 years. After pre primary school the children have to take admission in lower primary school.
  • Upper primary education: The children aged between 9 and 11 years joining upper primary school. Grade 4 to 6 covers under upper primary education level.
  • Junior secondary education: Junior secondary education level includes 3 years grade 7, grade 8 and grade-9. After completion of junior secondary school the students were promoted to senior secondary school.
  • Senior secondary education: The children aged between 15 and 17 years comes under senior secondary education level.
  • Higher education: The higher education level includes three years. After completing secondary education level the students join higher education.

National goals of education in Kenya

The new government of Kenya found that it is necessary to revise the whole school curriculum and define clearly the goals of education in an independent state. In 1964 by the Kenya Education Commission, this important task was first undertaken.

There are six national goals of education in Kenya are outlined by the Kenya Education Commission which was expected to fulfil by education system. After revision the eight national goals of education in Kenya are explained by the Kenya Institute of Education (KIE, 2002). The goals are as follows:

  • To promote economic, social, industrial and technological needs for national development: In the life of the nation, the youth of the country plays an effective role with the help of education.
  • Economic needs- To support a growing economy, Education in Kenya should produce children with knowledge, personal qualities, expertise and skills.
  • Social needs- For smooth process of a rapidly developing modern economy, Education in Kenya should teach students to adapt the changes.
  • Technological and industrial needs- With necessary attitudes and skills for industrial development the learners were provided by the Education in Kenya.
