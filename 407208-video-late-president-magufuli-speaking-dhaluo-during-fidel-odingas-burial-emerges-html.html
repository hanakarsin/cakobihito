Video of Late President Magufuli Speaking in Dhaluo During Fidel Odinga's Burial Emerges

- Magufuli excited mourners at Fidel Odinga's burial in Bondo, when he finished delivering his speech in Dholuo

- Raila and Magufuli met in South Africa sometime in 2003 when Odinga had just assumed office as Minister for Roads, Public Works, and Housing

- Another notable personality at Fidel's burial was former Zimbabwe prime minister Morgan Tsvangirai

A video of late Tanzanian President John Pombe Magufuli exchanging pleasantries with mourners during the burial of ODM party leader Raila Odinga's son, Fidel Castro Odinga has emerged online.

Speaking in Kang'o Ka Jaramogi in Bongo, January 2015, Magufuli excited mourners when he greeted them in the native Dholuo language at the tail end of his speech.

"Oyaore (how are you doing?) U ngima (are you fine?)," said Magufuli before he handed the microphone to Siaya Senator James Orengo who was the master of the ceremony during the burial.

Magufuli died on Wednesday, March 17, 2021.

He served as Minister of Works, Transport and Communications from 2000 to 2005 and 2010 to 2015 and was chairman of the Southern African Development Community from 2019 to 2020.

It was at an international conference on infrastructure in Durban, South Africa sometime in 2003 when Odinga had just assumed office as Minister for Roads, Public Works and Housing in the National Rainbow Coalition (Narc) government of President Mwai Kibaki that he and Magufuli met for the first time.

"By that time, Magufuli had held a similar portfolio for some time in Tanzania," Odinga said recently while mourning Magufuli.

Raila said that Magufuli took immense interest in his presentation while in Durban.

"He was particularly intrigued by my admission that corruption had found a home in the ministry and it was denying the country the good infrastructure needed for economic growth," added Odinga.

During the burial of Fidel, Magufuli was accompanied by a delegation of 13 people from Tanzania to condole with Raila Odinga and all other Kenyans.

"President Jakaya Kikwete has sent me with his condolence message. Fidel as you all know he was loved by many reflecting the number of mourners who have arrived here for his final send-off," Magufuli had said.

He urged all those present at the funeral to in peace and harmony with each other and also emulate Fidel.

"Former President Benjamin Mkapa has also send his condolences. We need to stand up together and protect the rights of Kenyans and Tanzanians and Africans as a whole," explained Magufuli.

Another notable personality at Fidel's burial was former Zimbabwe prime minister Morgan Tsvangirai.

Until his death in 2018, Tsvangirai was President of the Movement for Democratic Change, and later the Movement for Democratic Change – Tsvangirai, and a key figure in the opposition to former President Robert Mugabe.
