Ben Githae Says Kenyans Will Praise Uhuru after His Exit: &quot;Uzuri Huonekana Mtu Akiondoka&quot;

- Kikuyu gospel musician Ben Githae strongly believes that Kenyans will miss Uhuru's leadership past 2022

- He said when one is in office, their work is never seen until they leave, stating that Uhuru will be hailed for his government's achievements

- A section of Kenyans has been criticising the artiste for convincing them to re-elect Uhuru, whose government they believe has not lived up to their expectations

Kenyans might appreciate President Uhuru Kenyatta's leadership immediately after leaving office, says Tano Tena hitmaker Ben Githae.

After receiving so much hate from Kenyans concerning his song that promoted the re-election of the Jubilee government in 2017, Githae has come out to defend himself, stating Uhuru has done enough for the country, which would only be seen after his exit.

Githae, while appearing on Citizen radio for an interview, said Kenya has a good bunch of hypocrites who have chosen to blind themselves against the works of the president.

"Sometimes, someone is missed after vacating their office. Uhuru will also be praised one day after his term. Kenyans are used to complain about almost everything," said Githae.

He urged Kenyans to be patient with the president as much good could be happening in the background.

"Kenyans should be patient. I'm sure his (Uhuru's) work will be appreciated after he is out of government," said Githae.

Kenyans have been criticising the artiste claiming he misled them into re-electing a regime that has been more of a disservice than service to them.

Last week, anger was directed to the artiste following a gridlock that rocked several major roads in Nairobi as curfew was being enforced.

Githae was faulted for vouching for a government, through his composition, that would later inconvenience its citizens through the anti-COVID-19 measures.

From their regrets, Kenyans remembered how the Kikuyu gospel musician convinced them to vote for the Jubilee government using his song.

Many blamed Githae saying his song messed up the country.

In his defence, the artiste has often reiterated that he did the Tano Tena song out of the strong belief in the Uhuru-Ruto duo, stating he is not responsible for the failures that followed the re-election of their government.
