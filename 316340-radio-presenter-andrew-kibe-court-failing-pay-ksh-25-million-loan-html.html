Radio presenter Andrew Kibe in court for failing to pay KSh 2.5 million loan

Popular radio presenter Andrew Kibe was on Wednesday, September 25, arraigned over an alleged failure to pay up a KSh 2.5 million loan.

Kibe, who has made a name for himself through his vulgar rants on social media, was initially supposed to pay KSh 215,000 which is said to have been borrowed from a former colleague.

The said colleague, who Citizen TV identified as Joshua Ichang’i Weru, claims to have loaned Kibe the money seven years ago and it was to be paid back with an interest of KSh 55,000.

The money was to be paid within 18 days.

“Failure to complete the above contractual obligation by February 28 2012 incurs an additional penalty of 7.5 % in the above mentioned interest for every four days exceeded from the disclaimer date,” court papers read.

The loan of KSh 215,000 accrued to the staggering KSh 2.5 million in the seven years since it was issued, a suit filed in 2018 against Kibe from Musyoki Mogaka & Co. Advocates indicated.

Because the suit was filed in June last year, the arrears might be higher than the quoted figure if the 7.5% interest is anything to go by.

The lawsuit compounds more trouble for Kibe, who only recently won another potential court battle which saw himself and colleague Kamene Goro barred from hitting the airwaves after their unceremonious exit from the fairly new NRG radio.

The issue was, however, solved amicably.
