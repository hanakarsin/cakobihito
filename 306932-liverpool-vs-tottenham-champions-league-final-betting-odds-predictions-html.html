Liverpool Vs Tottenham: Champions League final betting odds and predictions

There is no exciting sporting event like the UEFA Champions League finals. This year, the competition is hotter, tighter, tougher, exciting and mind-blowing. Two of England’s finest will be locking horns at the Wanda Metropolitano stadium in Madrid, Spain. The Liverpool Vs Tottenham match will kick off on Saturday 1st June 2019 at 2200 hours. Numerous features have been introduced in this year’s finals including the Virtual Assisted Referee (VAR), and an additional substitute that will be allowed at extra time. With all the excitement and zeal ahead of the match, there is nothing that can stop you from watching this historical football fixture.

All systems are already set ahead of the Champions League final. This match will be the final fixture in the 2018-19 UEFA Champions League campaign and will bring together Premier League rivals Liverpool and Tottenham Hotspurs. It will be the 27th season of the premier club football which was rebranded to UEFA Champions League. Spurs will be reaching their first ever European cup final while the Reds will be reaching their ninth final and the second in a row.

This is the seventh ever Champions League final to have both teams from the same league play in the finals. It will also be the first final playing since 2013 with no Spanish team represented, as the last five titles were shared among Real Madrid and Barcelona. Winner of this cup will walk away with tons of goodies, among them will be playing the 2019 super cup against the winners of the Europa League.

Champions League final 2019 venue and date

The long-awaited finals will be going down this Saturday at 2200 hours EAT. Unlike most finals which have been playing at 9:45 pm, UEFA announced the change of time while also introducing the use of VAR for all finals going forward.

The venue for the event will be at the Wanda Metropolitano, the home ground for Spanish La Liga first runners up, Atletico Madrid. Champions League final has been held in Madrid four times, (1957, 1969, 1980, and 2010) ever since its inception.

Liverpool Vs Tottenham Champions League history

Tottenham has already made a club history to reach the finals of a major European cup final. It will be their first time to ever reach the Champions League final and the fortieth unique team from England to reach this level of the competition. Should they go on and win the finals on Saturday, the club will join the European giants. This includes Manchester United, Bayern Munich, Chelsea, Juventus and Ajax, who have won all the three major trophies in the European Leagues. In the European cup matches against fellow English teams, Spurs have had four wins, three losses and one draw in all the competitions.

Liverpool has reached their overall ninth Champions League final and two in a row after losing to Real Madrid in the last campaign. They are the five-time winners of the trophy for the years 1977, 1978, 1981, 1984, and 2005. They have also lost three including last season’s finals (1985, 2007, and 2018). Cumulatively, the club has played a total of 14 finals at the European level after playing four Europa League finals where they won in 1973, 1976, and 2001; lost in 2016.

Out of 20 matches where they met English competitors, Liverpool has won seven times, lost five times, and had eight draws. In the most recent encounter, the club won against Manchester city on both legs of the Champions League quarterfinals. If they win this year’s finals, it will be Jürgen Klopp’s first Champions League win after losing twice at Borussia Dortmund in 2013, and at Liverpool in 2018.

Liverpool Vs Tottenham results history

Their encounter at the finals will be the 172nd competitive match between these two English side teams. Of these encounters, Liverpool has been the better team emerging with a record of 82 wins against 48 wins for Tottenham, with only 41 draws between the two in all competitions. In their latest encounter during the 2018-19 Premier League, Liverpool won 2-1 in both their home and away fixture at Spurs.

The teams have also faced each other once in the semi-final of the 1972-73 UEFA Cup where Liverpool won the first leg by 1-0. Tottenham won the second leg 2-1, but the Reds proceeded to the cup finals on away goal advantage. In the domestic scene, the two clubs have met only once in the 1982 Football League Cup Final where Liverpool emerged winners with 3-1 after an extra time play.

There is no better glory for any European club that compares to winning the Champions League trophy. It is considered as one of the most prestigious trophies in the world and the highest rated competition in the European sports scene. As from 2018-2019, the preliminary amount paid per club is as follows:

  • Qualifying round amount: €230,000 (equivalent to Kshs. 26 million)
  • First round qualifiers: €280,000 (Kshs 31million)
  • Second round qualifiers: €380,000 (Kshs 43 million)
  • Third qualifying round: €480,000 (Kshs 54 million)
  • Group stage Base fee: €15,250,000 (Kshs 1.7 billion)
  • Victory per Group match: €2,700,000 (Kshs 305 million)
  • Draw per Group match amount: €900,000 (Kshs 101 million)
  • Round of 16 amount: €9,500,000 (Kshs 1.07 billion)
  • Quarter-finals amount: €10,500,000 (Kshs 1.18 billion)
  • Semi-finals amount: €12,000,000 (Kshs 1.35 billion)
  • Losing finalist amount: €15,000,000(Kshs 1.7 billion)
  • Champions League Winner prize: €19,000,000 (Kshs 2.15 billion)

From this distribution, a club can win up to €82,450,000 (Ksh 9.32 billion) in prizes, without factoring shares in the qualifying rounds, market pool, and play-off rounds.

The largest part of the UEFA Champions League revenue mostly comes the market pool where the distribution is determined by the nation’s television market share. For instance, when Juventus emerged as the runners up in the 2014-2015 season, the club earned a whopping €89.1 million in total, where the prize money was only €30.9 million. This is compared to Barcelona, who earned €61.0 million the same period with prize money totalling to €36.4 million despite winning the overall prize.

How Tottenham reached the final

After the 2017-2018 EPL season, Tottenham ended up in the third team and qualified for the Champions League according to the UEFA qualifiers policy. They were pooled in group B alongside Dutch champions PSV Eindhoven, Barcelona, and Inter Milan of Italy. Spurs commenced their title race with an away match at the San Siro where they lost 2-1 after conceding two late goals. Their next match was against Barcelona at Webley, and again they lost 4-2 in favour of the Catalans. Their third match ended 2-2 against PSV.

In the return leg matches, they won twice against PSV and Inter Millan in a 2-1, and 1-0 respectively. They closed their pool matches with a 1-1 draw against Barcelona and were tied on points with Inter Milan. However, they qualified for the next round as runners up due to the head-to-head away goals advantage.

In the round of 16, Spurs faced German side club, Borussia Dortmund in a match that ended 3-0 at Spurs where the home team won. The second leg encounter was played at Westfalenstadion in Dortmund, where the score ended at 1-0 favouring the visitors. Cumulatively, Spurs emerged the overall winners with an aggregate of four goals, sending them to the quarterfinals of the competition.

In the quarterfinals, Spurs were drawn against bitter rivals and English side Champions Manchester City. Tottenham were the hosts of the first leg which ended 1-0 in favour of Spurs, a good opener for their new Tottenham Hotspur Stadium. The second leg was played in Etihad, in what was described as the match of the season. The game ended 4-3 in favour of the hosts, but Tottenham advanced to the semifinals on account of away goals.

In the semi-finals, Spurs were drawn against the resurgent Dutch side, Ajax, the four-time winners of the European Cup. With Harry Kane missing in the squad, Spurs lost their home-based fixture 1-0 and dimmed their chances of proceeding to the finals. However, the hat trick by Moura during the second leg was a turning point for the Spurs as the match ended 2-3 in favour of Spurs. Tottenham escaped with the win narrowly on account of away goals advantage, leaving the Dutch champions heartbroken as they were the favourites coming into the match.

How Liverpool reached the finals

After emerging as the runners up in last season’s campaign, Liverpool automatically qualified for the Champions League slot. They were pooled against Paris Saint Germaine, Napoli, and Red Star Belgrade. After playing six matches, they emerged with 9 points and tied with Napoli. However, they advanced to the round of 16, thanks to the many goals they had scored.

At 16, the Reds matched against Bayern Munich, where they emerged with a 0-0 draw in the first leg and a 3-1 win in the second leg in favour of Liverpool. The Reds were back to their winning ways when they were matched against the Portuguese champions, Porto. Liverpool won 6-1 on aggregate where the first leg ended in a 2-0 brace and the second leg saw a 4-1 stunner in the Reds’ favour.

The semi-final was the epitome of their campaign as the Reds were matched against already crowned La Liga champions, Barcelona. In what seemed like an obvious outcome match, Barcelona took advantage of Liverpool’s defence weaknesses and capitalized on it with a 3-0 victory. This match was occasioned by mixed reactions after Messi scored his 600th goal for the Catalans through a 25-yard free kick in the 82nd minute.

The second leg was an almost obvious walk over for Barcelona as Liverpool had a 3-0 deficit to equalize and score a new goal if they were to qualify. Liverpool’s Jürgen Klopp was quoted imploring his players to "just try" or "fail in the most beautiful way." Liverpool managed to outperform the Catalans and overturn the deficit emerging with a 4-0 win at Anfield. The final aggregate was a 4-3 score, and Liverpool was once again on the road to the finals.

Liverpool Vs Tottenham highlights of the season

In the just concluded 2018/2019 EPL campaign, the two teams were phenomenal in their performances. Liverpool emerged the first runners up after Manchester city with a whopping 97 points, just one point behind the Champions. Further, they have lost only one match in the entire 38 fixtures, winning 30, drawing seven times and scoring 89 goals.

Tottenham put a spirited fright throughout the entire season to emerge fourth with 71 points, just one behind third-place Chelsea. They won 21 matches, lost 13, and emerged with two draws. With a goal difference of 28, Spurs are already qualified for the Champions League next season, on condition that Chelsea wins the Europa League finals.

The teams are on the brink of making history, and each manager wants to add achievement in management portfolios. The two teams will be bringing nothing but the exemplary performance and quality football.

Liverpool Vs Tottenham prediction

After all is said, here is our analysis for the match and the betting tips for you. All teams are fully composed and on top of their form. The managers have expressed their feelings on preparations ahead of the match. However, there are still worries about the return of Harry Kane, but that does not seem to stop the Spurs’ ambitions. They have already won the semifinals against resilient Ajax without him and would do it again.

Liverpool, on the other hand, has been here already and are better experienced at this level of the competition. Despite displaying a great play in the 2017/2018 finals against Real Madrid, they still lost owing to the poor performance of their then goalkeeper, Loris Karius. This time, they are back on the pitch with both confidence and experience they gained from the last UCL finals encounter.

After 172 encounters between these two sides, Liverpool has emerged winners 82 times against Tottenham’s 48 times, and 42 draws. In their meetings last season, Tottenham lost both home and away 1-2, and 2-1 respectively.

Based on the performance of the teams and history, we predict that there are better chances in favour of the Reds.
