Phenomenal and Standout Woman Trailblazer, Uhuru Mourns Orie Rogo Manduli

  • President Uhuru Kenyatta mourned Manduli as a woman of many firsts and trailblazer who fought tirelessly for women empowerment
  • In his message of condolence, the head of state described Manduli as a fearless, bold and practical leader who always spoke her mind
  • Uhuru said Manduli fully embraced the African culture and was a recognised fashion icon and a trendsetter

President Uhuru Kenyatta has joined Kenyans to mourn the death of veteran politician and fashion icon Orie Rogo Manduli, who passed away on the evening of Wednesday, September 9, at her Nairobi home.

Woman of many firsts

Uhuru mourned Manduli as a woman of many firsts and trailblazer who fought tirelessly for women empowerment in Africa.

“It is sad and unfortunate that death has taken away Mama Orie Rogo Manduli, one of our country's most recognizable, highly charismatic and accomplished politicians. A bold leader and mentor, Manduli tirelessly championed for women empowerment,” the president said.

Uhuru added the late was a fearless, bold and practical leader who always spoke her mind.

Manduki broke barriers

He recalled the late Manduli's exploits as a sportswoman and model, saying Kenyans will forever cherish her achievement as the first African woman Safari Rally driver, a feat she achieved when she took part in the 1974 edition alongside her late co-driver Sylvia Omino.

“The late Orie Rogo Manduli was a woman of many firsts who never shied away from exploring new horizons. She took up and excelled in motorsports at a time when many African women would never have dared, thereby opening up the sport to women drivers in later years.At the tender age of 16 years, while still in secondary school, Manduli won the Miss Kenya beauty pageant. She was simply a phenomenal and standout woman trailblazer,” Uhuru said.

On her signature attire and headgear, the head of state said the departed Orie Rogo fully embraced the African culture and was a recognised fashion icon and trendsetter.

“Mama Orie Rogo Manduli was a fashion icon whose trademark African print attire and prominent head gear made her one of Africa's most recognizable personalities.”

Manduli's death was confirmed by her family, who disclosed the renowned politician died at her home in Nairobi.

However, it was not immediately clear what led to her death.

In the history books, Manduli would be most recognised as the first Kenyan woman rally driver. Her iconic fashion made her unique and expensive fashion designs made her stand out among all the female political figures.
