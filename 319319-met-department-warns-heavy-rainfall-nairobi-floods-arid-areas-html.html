Met department warns of heavy rainfall in Nairobi, floods in arid areas

- Kenya Meteorological Department listed Nairobi, Central and North East as places likely to experience heavy rains

- The department said the wet weather conditions will continue throughout the week until Sunday 20

- Residents are asked to avoid driving through surface run-offs, walking in moving water or open fields

Wet weather conditions currently experienced over several parts of the country are expected to continue even as the Kenya Meteorological Department (KMD) warned of more heavy rainfall.

According the latest weather update, Nairobi, North East, Central, South East and Coastal regions will on Wednesday, October 16, record heavy rainfall of more than 20mm in 24 hours which will double from Thursday 17.

Residents living in the above areas have been advised to be on the look out for potential floods as flash floods are likely to occur in even in areas where it has not rained heavily especially in semi-arid places and urban centres.

The weatherman also reminded road users to avoid driving through surface run-offs, walking in moving water or open fields.

The heavy rains and moderate and strong winds may result in storm surges along the coast hence fishermen and all in the marine industry should be on high alert at any time.
