Mombasa Road: KeNHA Says All Closed Sections Will Be Opened on October 15

  • The team would be charged with ensuring that construction sites along the Nairobi Expressway have adequate and well-signposted signs
  • Major ongoing road work concentrated along the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) and Mlolongo section causes heavy traffic jam along the road
  • Other sections of the road that are still under construction include Westlands-James Gichuru and Haile Selassie-UON Roundabout

Motorists using Mombasa Road can breathe a sigh of relief after the Kenya National Highway Authority (KeNHA) announced the projected dates for the closed road sections to ease the current traffic situation in Nairobi.

KeNHA has at the same time announced the formation of a multi-agency team to solve the traffic menace on Mombasa Road.

In a statement seen by TUKO.co.ke on Tuesday, August 31, KeNHA said the team would be charged with ensuring that construction sites along the Nairobi Expressway have adequate and well-signposted signs.

''In light of the recent traffic flow disruptions and delays along Mombasa Road, KeNHA is determined to reduce the time spent on this road to the bare minimum,'' the statement signed by KeNHA Acting Director-General David Muchilwa reads in part.

The state agency attributed the huge traffic snarl-ups on Mombasa Road over the weekend to the major ongoing road work concentrated along the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) and Mlolongo section.

''There has been recorded positive progress on the earthworks along this JKIA-Mlolongo section of the road. This section was earlier identified as one where motorists experienced extreme traffic congestion,'' the statement further reads.

KeNHA said the contractor is committed to minimising dust along the construction sites and the diversion through sprinkling.

''However, motorists are urged to avoid driving on the construction site and follow the designated diversion to avoid raising dust,'' said Muchilwa.

According to KeNHA, the projected dates for the closed JKIA-Mlolongo section is September 10, 2021, Westlands-James Gichuru (September 30, 2021), Haile Selassie-UON Roundabout (October 15, 2021), while the Elevated section (viaduct) stands at 71.6% completion.

Frustrated Kenyan motorists

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, hundreds of Kenyans plying the ever-busy Mombasa Road were on Thursday, August 26, forced to ''sleep'' on the road following a massive gridlock.

The heavy traffic started building up from Thursday morning and intensified towards the night in a move that saw many Kenyans get to their destinations in the wee hours.

On Monday, August 30, KeNHA appealed to all motorists to exercise caution while driving along Mombasa Road and keep within the speed limits indicated along with other traffic signs.
