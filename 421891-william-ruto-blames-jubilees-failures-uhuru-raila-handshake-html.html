William Ruto Blames Jubilee's Failures on Uhuru-Raila Handshake

  • William Ruto said Jubilee had wasted four years of his second term focusing on BBI instead of Big Four Agenda
  • He said the government lost its way when it joined hands with Opposition principals after the handshake
  • A week ago, Senator Murkomen said Ruto should not be blamed for Jubilee failures because the real DP is Raila Odinga

Deputy President William Ruto has blamed Jubilee government's failures on President Uhuru Kenyatta's truce with Opposition leader Raila Odinga.

According to Ruto, the government neglected the Big Four Agenda after joining hands with Raila and his former moribund National Super Alliance (NASA) principals, whom he claimed prioritised amending the constitution.

In a video shared by Daily Nation, the second in command said technically, Jubilee had wasted four years of its reign focusing on the handshake and the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI).

"Unfortunately, we did not have an opportunity to deliver on our Big Four agenda because we were told by NASA principals that changing the constitution was an emergency.They wanted more positions and power to share. That's why for the last four years, instead of the Big Four, we have been involved in reggae, and changing the constitution," he said adding that Jubilee had lost four years.

Working with Raila

A couple of months ago, Uhuru hit out at his deputy saying that he had done better in his second team in terms of development than in the first term of presidency.

Uhuru divulged that he had focused more on development in his second term than in the first term, where he focused more on politicking.

“Working in conjunction with my colleagues, I have been able to focus on the development agenda, not the political agenda,” the president said in June 2021.

Uhuru and Raila shook away their political animosity away on the steps of Harambee House Nairobi on March 8, 2018, in what came to be known as the handshake.

The handshake, however, threw Ruto and his allies to the periphery while Raila got a seat at the table; it also exposed weaknesses in the ruling Jubilee Party which fragmented into various factions.

Defacto deputy president

A week ago, Elgeyo Marakwet senator Kipchumba Murkomen said Ruto should be absolved from the Jubilee government's failures.

The Tanga Tanga senator said the Opposition leader has been the real deputy president and fingers should therefore be pointed to him.

"William Ruto is not part of this administration. He is the dejure but defacto, he is not in office. The real deputy president at the moment is Raila Odinga," he said.
