Gov't Reminder on Suspension of All Public Gatherings Attracts Backlash: &quot;Were Tired&quot;

  • Cyrus Oguna's reminder on the ban of political gatherings irked a number of Kenyans online
  • Many said the government spokesperson should warn politicians directly because they are the ones at the frontline in the breaking of COVID-19 rules
  • This came after Raila Odinga and William Ruto attracted mega crowds in two separate events this week

A government reminder on suspension of public gatherings has sparked outrage from a section of Kenyans online.

Through his Twitter page, Government Spokesperson Cyrus Oguna said all political rallies remain suspended to curb the transmission of COVID-19 in the country.

However, in the comment section, many Kenyans expressed their disappointment, questioning why the message was not directed to the politicians as they have been holding gatherings in the recent past.

"Arrest the politicians holding those Political gatherings and stop killing youths because of curfew," he said.

Raila in Nakuru

This came just a day after ODM leader Raila Odinga stormed Nakuru where he attracted a big crowd during his visit.

As his entourage entered the town, Raila was received by some residents chanting his name 'Baba Baba Baba', according to a video shared on his Facebook page.

While addressing residents, the 2022 presidential hopeful insisted the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) was on the course ahead of the Court of Appeal judgment on Friday, August 20.

Ruto in Kawangware

On Sunday, August 15, Deputy President William Ruto also attracted a large crowd in Kawangwe after a church service at the St Francis of Assisi Catholic Church.

During the service, the deputy president donated KSh 1 million to the church for its expansion plan.

Taking to the podium, Ruto challenged his competitors, who he claimed were planning to gang against him in the upcoming vote.

He said the team led by ODM leader Raila Odinga and the One Kenya Alliance (OKA) luminaries had no agenda to solve the problems ailing the common man, instead were eyeing positions of power.
