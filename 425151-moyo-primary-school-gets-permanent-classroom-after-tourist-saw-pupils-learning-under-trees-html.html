Moyo Primary School Gets Permanent Classrooms After Tourist Saw Pupils Learning Under Trees

  • A tourist was forced to change the course of her visit to Kenya when she bumped into a school whose pupils were learning under trees
  • Seeing the plight of the children, she started fundraising for a structure and recently built the school its first permanent classroom block
  • Three weeks since the classroom was built, the student population has risen from 25 to 75

For years, pupils at Moyo Primary School in Kajiado county were accustomed to attending classes under trees due to a lack of classrooms.

There were no desks, no blackboard, and no stationery, so the learners had to make do with jotting notes in the soil.

"It was chaos whenever it rained because learning would not happen. So during the rainy season, we all stayed at home," Janet Saloni, a teacher at the school told TV47.

Saloni added then the weather elements caused such disturbances, the teachers would be forced to dismiss the students and call it a day.

Built permanent structures

That will soon be a thing of the past, thanks to a kind tourist who witnessed their plight firsthand and decided to better their lives.

According to sources, the tourist bumped into a class that was in session under a tree, something that moved her.

"So when she raised funds to construct classrooms, we joined forces and donated desks," said one of the community members.

Other well-wishers and corporates got on board the course and helped resource the school, among them Crown Paints.

According to a parent Nalua Ntuki, the school started in 2019 with 14 students to fill the education gap as children of school-going age had nowhere to receive formal education.

The school now boasts three classroom blocks, permanent administration offices, and good toilets.

Records indicate that since the school was reopened with new infrastructure, there has been an increase in the number of students from 25 to 75.

Jude Bellingham builds school in Mombasa

In an almost similar story, Borussia Dortmund star Jude Bellingham recently helped raise funds to build a school that would accommodate 275 pupils in Mombasa, Kenya

The English international used his social media accounts along with Mustard Seed Project to help finish the project.

That was the second time he was running a project to help Kenya as he had previously helped purchase football kits for youngsters from the coastal city's slums.
