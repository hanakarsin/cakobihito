Mwalimu Dida Tells Men to Confirm if Money They Lend Women is Refundable: &quot;Know Borrowing Terms&quot;

- Abduba Dida said men should know the borrowing terms before sending money to their female friends, whether in love or not

- He argued that it would be proper for any man to normalised asking if the money he was about to send would be refundable

- The teacher turned politician urged men not to be ashamed of being referred to as mean

Since time immemorial, it has always been believed that men should be the ones to provide for their women.

In the Kenyan context, there is a special breed of women who believe men should cater for the transport money when she is visiting. The same man is also supposed to pay for the hotel bill.

There is another group of women that would ask for money from their male friends whenever they are in a financial fix and promise to pay in one or two weeks.

In most cases, they never refund and as a man, you are supposed to act gently.

According to former presidential candidate Mwalimu Abduba Dida, men should know the borrowing terms before sending money to their female friends, whether in love or not.

He argued that it would be proper for any man to normalise asking if the money he is about to wire to the female's account would be refundable and if so, when.

"Sons, normalise asking your (female) friends, “...is it refundable? And when?”, before lending them money. Know the terms of her borrowing. These loans are highly defaulted. And don’t you worry about being called mean, that is another term for financially responsible," he advised.

In a separate but related story, controversial relationships expert Benjamin Zulu termed as stupid men who send transport money to women who never show up.

"If you are being sent fare you are not fit for dating. You are fit for playing," said Zulu.

Zulu reiterated that when a woman receives the fare from a man, it makes her indebted to the sender as he expects something in return.

"Look for people who are in your league. I am building an empire I need an empress. A woman who can think large" added Zulu.
