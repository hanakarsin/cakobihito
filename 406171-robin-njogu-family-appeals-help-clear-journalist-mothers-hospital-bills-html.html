Njogu breathed his last on Tuesday, March 16, after a long and painful battle with COVID-19 at The Aga Khan Hospital in Nairobi.

The revered journalist's demise occurred barely two days after the death of his mother.

His family said the hospital bill has accumulated into millions of shillings and were planning for a fundraiser at the Sagret Hotel located in Milimani on Thursday, March 18.

The exercise will be held at 5.30 pm.

You can stand with the family using Safaricom's money transfer service M-Pesa, Paybill Number 4068000 and Account Number, your name

The journalist was the managing editor of the radio news at Royal Media Services.

As a trained fact-checker, Njogu's constant tweets proved useful for to his followers and those looking to debunk fake news.

The stellar scribe previously worked with Capital FM from 1999 to 2012 as a news editor before he moved to the Nation Media Group to head the radio news section.

In 2015, he joined the Royal Media Services to become the managing director, a position that saw him being in charge of 13 radio stations until his death.
