Meet 79-year-old man who claims to have sold DP Ruto first pair of shoes in 1980's

The country was left in awe when Deputy President William Ruto revealed that his father bought him the first pair of shoes in 1981 from a local shoe vendor he identified as Onyango.

Days after the revelation, the 79-year-old man who still works as a cobbler in Eldoret's Ronald Ngala street has shown up.

Albert Onyango, who appeared on KTN News said he sold the shoes to young Ruto in early 1980's and never knew the boy would become a great man in the county.

"He came with his father and I sold them the pair of shoes without knowing the young man would become a leader in the county," recalled Onyango

He said since the then, he has neither met Ruto nor his father because he treated them as normal customers whom he hoped would come again.

The aging man revealed he was elated to hear that Ruto still recalled where he got his first pair of shoes.

"I had him on radio and I was really happy, I am hoping to meet him in person one day," said Onyango

He pleaded with Ruto to help him boost his business which he has operated for over forty years now.

During the National Prayer Breakfast, the DP said his father bought him the shoes at a cost of KSh 60 since he could not afford to buy other expensive brands.

"When I went to do my Standard Seven exams, I was barefoot because that was the nature of village life. I first wore shoes in 1981 which my father bought me from a Mr Onyango at KSh 60 because he could not afford Bata shoes," he said.
