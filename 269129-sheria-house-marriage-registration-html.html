After all, there’s nothing as wonderful as getting married, and you do not want yours to be an exception by breaking the law.

The minimum age allowed for marriage by the constitution of Kenya is 21 years of age. However, ages slightly lower than this are still allowed provided there was parental consent. The groom must have attained the age of minimum age of 18 years, and the bride must be 16 years and over. Notably, the constitution doesn’t have a provision for same-sex marriage. Therefore, it’s not possible to have a legally recognized marriage ceremony when both parties to the union are of similar gender.

There are to forms of wedding unions in that are legally recognized in Kenya: Religious and Civil marriage ceremonies. The ceremony can either be presided over by a civil official or an authorized religious leader, and it takes place at the chosen location. This event comes before you proceed to the office of the Registrar of Marriages to file the Certificate of Marriage. It is only after these conditions have been met that the marriage becomes fully recognized by the law of the land.

The necessary documents and requirements to register for marriage

If it’s a marriage between Kenyan citizens, the couple will be required to provide copies of national identification cards, a colored passport size photos for both parties, and a fee of Kshs.600. In case the wedding ceremony takes place at the registrar’s office, you’ll pay a fee of Kshs.3,300 before you’re given the marriage date as per the official diary. If you want to go the church way, you’ll pay Kshs.800. In case the wedding takes place in other locations apart from the Registrar’s office or church, you will need a Special license for the venue at Kshs.7,200.

In case it’s a marriage between a foreign national and a Kenyan, the Kenyan party will provide original and copies of the national identification card or valid Kenyan passport, a colored passport size photo, an affidavit, and an original and copies of the birth certificate. The foreign national, on the other hand, will present an original and copies of their valid passport and birth certificate as well as a colored passport photo an a Certificate of No Impediment to Marriage. This includes a death certificate if the party is widowed, or an original or copy of the Divorce Decree Absolute. Finally, they will need to provide their work permit, visa, or return air ticket, and both parties to the marriage should be present.

First, you will need to visit the Attorney General’s office with your spouse at Sheria House. You will bring with you both your original copies of your national identity cards or passports. You will then complete the Notice of intention to marry form Kenya and pay Ksh.200. Notably, this notice usually runs for three weeks (21 days).

In the event that one or both parties to the union are widowed, you will need to produce the original and copies of the Death Certificate for the departed spouse. On the other hand, in the event that one or both parties to the marriage are divorced, you will be required to produce a Divorce Decree Absolute. Should these documents be in any other language and not English, you will be required to attach an official English translation.

Once the 21-day notice has elapsed, the couple will complete the affidavit under Section 11 of the Marriage Act. You will then get this affidavit commissioned by the Registrar of marriages Kenya before completing the Marriage Certificate Form. Once done, you will then be required to pay Ksh.1,500 before proceeding to book a date for your marriage. Notably, it is a requirement that the marriage takes place within three months. This countdown commences immediately after the date you gave the 21-day notice.

Special license

In the event that the parties cannot provide the 21-day notice for a very good reason, the Registrar of Marriages may as well waive the notice. Notably, this can be denied since it is not a right. Here are the requirements for the special license:

  • You will need to provide valid passports or identity cards which serve as the only forms of identification that are valid. In case a person possesses any other type of identification, they must provide the 21-day notice.
  • Widowed parties must provide the Death certificate, and those who are divorced are required to provide a divorce Decree absolute.
  • Alternatively, you can provide written evidence and explanation on why you can’t provide the notice. For instance, an applicant can provide an embassy invitation letter inviting them for an interview scheduled on a specific date.
  • A completed application form

  • The cost: If the civil wedding is between two Kenyan citizens, you will be required to pay a fee of Kshs.600 as you visit the office of the registrar of marriages for the first time. The initial visit is usually meant to deliver a notice of the marriage which should be done 21 days prior to the stipulated marriage date. After this notice, you will be required to pay a fee of Kshs.3,300, and you will be offered a wedding date on which you would set your wedding ceremony.

Normally, civil wedding celebrations are usually conducted from 9.00am to 3.00am on every weekday that comes after the 21-day notice has expired. In fact, by the time you arrive, you will find several other couples in the queue.
  • On arrival: Once you get to the office of the Registrar at Sheria House, you will be allocated a number on the queue, and you will be asked to wait for your turn from the common waiting area. Once it’s your turn, you will be called to one of the rooms where you are required to provide all details of your witnesses. You will also confirm all details as captured on the certificate soft copy before the actual printing.
  • At the waiting room: You will then be ushered to another waiting room next to the room set aside for the wedding alongside your guests and witnesses. Notably, you are allowed to bring a minimum of two witnesses and a maximum of 15. Here, you will have access to another changing room where brides and grooms can change into their wedding attire or apply make up to glam their civil marriage ceremony. You can’t help but notice the excitement in all the people present at this location!
  • The ceremony: Once it’s your turn, an officer will call out your names and those of your guests. You will then access the marriage room on one door as the couple that was right ahead of you leaves through another door. The couple and the two witnesses will sit in the front seats, and the ceremony will now commence.
  • The vows: You will be asked to confirm your intention and decision to get married before repeating the vows after the registrar. The registrar will then ask for the rings (of course, you’ll bring your rings with you!) and the groom will be the first one to repeat his vows. The groom will then be requested by the registrar to pale the marriage ring on his bride’s finger as the guests clap at their own pleasure. The bride will also do the same before the magical ‘you may now kiss the bride’ phrase is mentioned.
  • Signing the marriage certificate: You will be issued with three hard copies of the marriage certificate to sign alongside your witnesses. At this point, your guests are allowed to sing, and the registrar will sign all copies of the certificates and issue two to the couple.
  • Some photos to keep the memory: You will then move to one decorated side of the room for the photo session. This will be followed by lots of congratulatory handshakes and hugs before it’s time for you to exit via the other door and the officer invites another couple in the room.

Special licenses and registrar certificates if you intend to get married in a church

If you intend to solemnize your marriage in church (under Cap 150 of the Marriage Act), you will give Notice to your church as well as the Office of the Registrar of Marriages. Once the notice timeline of 21 days elapses, the Office of the Registrar of Marriages will issue a Registrar’s Certificate, which will be submitted to the Church Minister or the person who is in charge of officiating the marriage. Notably, you will be charged Kshs.200 for the Notice and Kshs.300 for the Registrar’s Certificate.

Customary marriage in Kenya - registration, divorce, procedure

The constitution of Kenya expressly provides for the protection of equality of marriage and family. The marriage act was enacted by parliament in 2014, and it provides for registration of all marriage systems. These include Customary, Hindu, Christian, Civil, and Islamic. This was done in accordance with all recommendations made by the Hon. Justice (Rtd) Effie Owuor-led taskforce.

The rules were established with the purpose of giving effect on the status equality between all marriage systems while providing a process in which marriages will be registered through the certificates that are issued at the office of the Registrar of Marriages. Here are the requirements that you should meet to register your customary marriage (as per Gazette Notice 5345)

  • The registration of this category of marriage will commence on August 1, 2017.
  • Couples that were joined under customary law are only required to register their marriage before being offered a certificate.
  • Parties intending to commit under Customary Marriages after the registration date commences shall notify the Registrar for them to be issued with a certificate. This should be within three months after completing the necessary, customary rites.
  • The phrase ‘Prior Authorization’ found in the Gazette Notice is used to refer to guidance for the purpose of commencement of Customary Marriage registration.

Where to get certificate of no impediment to marriage

These certificates are usually given to Kenyan citizens who wish to celebrate their marriage, whether to either a fellow Kenyan citizen or to foreigners, from abroad. This certificate is issued to confirm that the applicants are single and have the capacity to be united through marriage. Applicants can are required to send a letter to the District Commissioner of their home district if their home district isn’t Nairobi.

In the letter, you will be requesting the DC to display a notice of the intended marriage union on their notice board or even carry out the relevant investigations through the Chief’s office in your home location on your marital status. The District Commissioner will then write to the registrar confirming that no one has raised a legal objection. Your certificate of no impediment to marriage will then be prepared in the normal way then dispatched via the normal channels.
