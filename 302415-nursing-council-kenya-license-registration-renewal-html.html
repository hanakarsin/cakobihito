Nursing Council of Kenya license registration and renewal

Nursing Council of Kenya (NCK) was established by the Nurses Act Cap 257 of the laws of Kenya as a statutory body to ensure effective nursing and midwife care. NCK guarantees best practices through quality education. Despite being the only professional regulatory body in Kenya for all nurses and midwives, the NCK also accredits all Nursing colleges and institutions in Kenya.

As part of its mandate, Nursing Council of Kenya is tasked with the issuing of license and registration of all trained nurses after passing the Council's national licensing examinations. This council performs the role of an oversight authority and accredits all nurses and midwifery training institutions.

License registration and renewal for nurses trained in Kenya

For one to be registered as a licensed nurse in Kenya, it is mandatory to sit and pass the national licensing examination. From here you will be required to apply for a license within 30 days after the results have been released by NCK. All successful applicants will be issued with a registration certificate and a practicing license.

Nurses trained outside Kenya (TOK)

Nurses trained outside Kenya, however, pass through different registration criteria. Applicants are required to attain all the levels and competency standards set by the Nursing Council of Kenya before contacting the NCK to initiate the registration process.

NCK will vet the applicant through evaluation of the training they received in their respective institutions. The registration and licensing committee will later contact successful applicants and invite them for an oral interview.

NCK will rely on the results of the oral interview to determine if the applicant can proceed to sit for the written exam. The applicant may also be required to undertake a clinical placement to acquaint themselves with the Kenyan health care systems for a specified period of time. An applicant will become eligible for registration and licensing after a successful campaign at the clinical placement.

Nursing Council of Kenya renewal of a nursing license

The renewal of a nursing license can be done online or by visiting the NCK offices personally. The applicant who wishes to renew their license will be required to pay a non-refundable renewal fee of Ksh. 2000 before filling the NCK form and submitting it to the NCK. The filled form should be accompanied by the following documents:

  • All Nursing Council of Kenya Registration Certificates.
  • National Identity Card [ID]/Alien [ID] and passport.
  • Two (2) coloured passport size photographs.
  • Expiring original nursing practice license
  • Evidence of having undergone 40 hrs of professional training/ CPD Log Book.
  • Payment receipt
  • The applicant will be required to wait for a period of 30 days for NCK to review their applications before approval and a new certificate will be issued once approved.
  • All applicants are supposed to produce original copies of their National ID or Passport as proof of their identity when they come to collect their renewed practising license.

Nursing Council of Kenya services

The NCK offers a variety of services, some which can be acquired via the online portal. The council also acts as an oversight to regulate and develop standard tools for education and training materials for institutions. You can access the renewal and licensing services, student indexing and clinical placement. The Nursing Council of Kenya keeps connected with all its members for continuous professional development and to ensure compliance and professionalism of the highest caliber.
