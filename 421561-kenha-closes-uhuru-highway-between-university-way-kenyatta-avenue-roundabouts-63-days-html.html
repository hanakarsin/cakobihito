Motorists using Uhuru Highway in Nairobi will be forced to find alternative routes after the Kenya National Highway Authority (KeNHA) closed parts of the road.

The authority on Friday, August 6, announced the closure of the road section between Kenyatta Avenue and the University Way roundabouts for 63 days, beginning August 9.

KeNHA director-general Peter Mundinia explained that the closure would enable the ongoing construction of the Nairobi expressway.

Mundinia advised motorists to use diversions as will be indicated or alternative routes to get to their destinations.

"The diversion shall entail the closure of three lanes for traffic heading to Westlands and the creation of two on the road reserve for traffic movement ....We apologise for any conveniences that may be caused as we endeavor to provide efficient, safe and reliable road network," he stated.

The 18.5km road starts at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport and ends at the James Gichuru junction along Waiyaki Way in Westlands.

The construction of the double-decker highway was initially set to begin in August 2019 but was pushed to October due to financial-related difficulties.

In January 2021, Transport Cabinet Secretary James Macharia gazetted the expressway as a toll road.

The gazettement means motorists will pay a fee to use the road, which will be completed in 2022.
