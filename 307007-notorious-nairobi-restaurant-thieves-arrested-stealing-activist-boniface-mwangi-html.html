Notorious Nairobi restaurant thieves arrested after stealing from activist Boniface Mwangi

Two thieves known for stealing from customers at restaurants in Nairobi have been arrested after they attempted to get away with activist Boniface Mwangi's bag.

Christopher Maina and Mary Wangechi were nabbed after they took the bag from the activist who was having coffee at a restaurant.

"I turned to remove my phone and saw them with my bag. I raised my voice and they were arrested by the restaurant security. They're at Central Police Station and guess what? They are wanted for theft in other restaurants," said Mwangi in a Facebook post.

He further disclosed that upon search on the bag they had they were found in possession of a roll of tissue paper which is believed as one of the items they use to stupor their victims.

However, it is not the first time the someone has attempted to steal from the activist given the recent incident where he lost his phone but it was returned by his driver.

The controversial phone theft dragged Mwangi into vitriol from a section of Kenyans who felt he was ungrateful to the taxi driver who had returned it to him despite having asked him about it earlier on.
