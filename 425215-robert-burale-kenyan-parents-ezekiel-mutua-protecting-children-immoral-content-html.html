Robert Burale Says Kenyan Parents Miss Ezekiel Mutua for Protecting Children from Immoral Content

  • The KFCB CEO Ezekiel Mutua was on August 6, asked to proceed on terminal leave
  • In his place, Christopher Wambua from the Communications Authority of Kenya was appointed in an acting capacity
  • Mutua's term was set to expire on October 25, but there is currently no information on what the future holds for him

Relationship coach, author and motivational speaker Robert Burale says that parents are missing the former Kenya Films and Classification Board (KFCB) CEO Ezekiel Mutua.

The stylish man of God said that some men and women who are all parents got talking about Mutua, and they all agreed on one thing, that they miss him.

We miss Ezekiel Mutua

" As a parent I sure miss Dr Mutua, he was doing alot to protect our children from some immoral content" one parent was quoted by Burale saying.

Burale noted that Kenyans could not take away Mutua's stand against some immoral content exposed to children.

"None of us is perfect but let us not deny good deeds done. Ezekiel Mutua, MBS God bless and may Heaven honour you. Sir get ready for a major open door," Burale posted on Facebook.

On August 6, Mutua was asked to proceed on terminal leave until the expiry of his term. This was his second term which expired on August 25.

The new development came hours after the moral police denied reports that he had been fired, calling the reports “malicious”. He said he was not aware of them.

Fight against dirty content

TUKO.co.ke previously reported that Mutua's fight against dirty content has never been personal or a witch-hunt.

According to Mutua, taking care of that now will ensure a future filled with artists who produce clean content.

"The campaign against dirty content is not a job or a personal matter. It's not us vs them. It's a collective effort to shape societal norms and values through art and entertainment. When the dust settles, let's remember that our future depends on the moral foundation we build today through conversations and stories in the media," Mutua posted on Facebook.
