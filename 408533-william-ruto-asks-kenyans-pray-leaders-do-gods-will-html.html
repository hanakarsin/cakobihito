William Ruto Asks Kenyans to Pray for Leaders to Do God's Will

- Deputy President William Ruto sent a special message to Muslims as they observed the holy month of Ramadhan

- The DP said the period was an opportunity for Muslims to unite with their counterparts from other denominations in praying for the country

- He also called on Kenyans to adhere to the COVID-19 protocols put in place by the government

Deputy President William Ruto has called on Kenyans to pray for their leaders as the country prepares for a major political showdown next year.

In his special message to Muslims as they marked the holy month of Ramadhan, Ruto urged Kenyans from other denominations to join the Islam community in praying for the nation.

He called for togetherness and unity among Kenyans at all times.

"Let's continue keeping the peace and unity because we are all one. Pray for all leaders irrespective of their political persuasions. Pray for us so that we can fulfil God's will in our country," he said.

The DP recognised the necessity of the Muslims' fasting period, adding it is a strong pillar in the religion.

"It is stated in the Quran that Allah says "O you who believe, fasting is prescribed for you as it was prescribed for those before you, that you may develop God-consciousness," said Ruto.

Ruto also took the chance to urge Kenyans to adhere to the protocols sanctioned by President Uhuru Kenyatta in a bid to combat the spread of COVID-19.

He said the directives were meant to counter the dreaded virus and also urged Kenyans to move to their nearby health facilities to receive the AstraZeneca COVID-19 jab.

Muslims began the 30-day long period of fasting on Monday, April 12.

During the period, they will be fasting from dusk to dawn, the aim being to get closer to God through prayer and contemplation.

As they abstain from food and drink all day, a pre-dawn meal called suhoor is served.

This year's fasting is slated to come to a halt on May 11 upon the sighting of the new moon.
