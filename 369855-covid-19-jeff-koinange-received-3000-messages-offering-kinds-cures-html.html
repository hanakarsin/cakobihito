COVID-19: Jeff Koinange says he received 3,000 messages, some offering all kinds of cures

- Jeff Koinange appeared on the JKL show on Wednesday night saying he believed he contracted the virus at the RMS' radio station

- The news anchor said he was feeling great seemingly because he was asymptomatic and had only lost a bit of taste while in self-Isolation

- Jeff decided to go public to help demystify stigma surrounding the virus and had received warm messages from Kenyans all over the world

- The radio personality said testing positive changed his perception of the virus, urging Kenyans to get tested

Jeff Koinange has shared his coronavirus story detailing how he believes he contracted the virus.

The Citizen TV news presenter appeared live on JKLive, hosted by colleague Victoria Rubadiri, and said he believed to have been infected while at the RMS' radio station.

This is because they were urged to get tested by their boss, Wachira Waruru, after someone at work tested positive and named him as his contact.

Jeff said he was feeling very good seemingly because he was asymptomatic and had only lost a bit of taste while in self-Isolation.

The media personality said going public was to help demystify the whole thing and had received warm messages with some advising him on all types of cures.

"Oh my God! On the first day, I had like 3,000 messages on WhatsApp, another 2,000 on SMS and tweets. Most of the people have been incredibly warm, they are praying for me, saying 'Come back strong, hang in there.' Others are offering all types of cure, they say 'Drink a lot of lemon water, ginger and garlic, take vitamin C, seat in the sun for like 20 minutes,"' he said.

Jeff also named some of the few people who called him to wish him a quick recovery including Githu Muigai, Kihara Kariuki and Francis Atwoli.

The newsman noted how his schedule had changed but his daily clock was still the same - as he still woke up at 4am.

"So I'm up, walking around the house now. Oh, the show has started, but I can not go back to bed and now till 10 o'clock my mind is programmed to radio," he said.

Jeff was keeping busy at home with some Netflix and taking walks around the house.

He was also hydrating a lot with the lemon, ginger mix and he would be back sooner than later.

He said testing positive changed his perception of the deadly virus, even applying health CS Mutahi Kagwe's words: "If you treat it normally, it will treat you abnormally."

"Treat it seriously, because this thing is no joke. A lot of people are walking out there and they probably have it and are in denial or they think it's going to go away if you can - get tested early so that you save yourself and those around you," Jeff added.

The Hot 96 radio host had revealed to have tested positive on Monday, July 20, saying his contacts had been informed.

He noted he was in isolation and thanked Royal Media Services' management for their support.
